⚖️ Rules Overview

Objective

Good team wins by executing the Demon before it kills everyone. Evil team wins when only 2 players remain alive (or through certain special conditions).

Teams

Players are secretly assigned a role. Townsfolk & Outsiders are Good. Minions & Demons are Evil. The Demon knows its Minions; Minions know the Demon.

Day Phase

Players discuss openly, share (or lie about) information, and eventually nominate players for execution. Each player may nominate once per day. A majority vote executes the nominee. Only one player can be executed per day.

Night Phase

All players close their eyes. The Storyteller wakes players in a set order so they may use their night abilities. The Demon chooses a kill target. Dead players may still participate in discussions but cannot vote or use abilities.

Death & Voting

Dead players keep their vote token but may only use it once for the rest of the game. Players who die at night are revealed the following morning. Executed players reveal their character immediately.

The Storyteller

The Storyteller runs the game: they wake players, deliver information, and can make judgment calls to keep the game fun and balanced. They may give slightly false information to poisoned or drunk players.

Poison & Drunk

A poisoned or drunk player's ability malfunctions: they still receive information but it may be false. They do not know they are affected. The Storyteller controls what false info is given.

Win Conditions

Good wins the moment the last Demon is executed or dies at night. Evil wins at the start of a day when only 2 players remain alive. Some roles have alternate win conditions — check the role card.

First Night vs. Other Nights: An asterisk (*) on a role means it acts on "other nights" — i.e. every night except the first. Roles without * may act on night 1 as well. The Storyteller follows a set order printed on the night sheet.

🎭 Roles

Townsfolk — Good

Townsfolk are the backbone of the good team. Their abilities gather information to find and execute the Demon. They must share and cross-reference clues to overcome Minion deception.

Chef Townsfolk

"You start knowing how many pairs of evil players are sitting adjacent to each other."

A count of 0 means no evil neighbors; a high count suggests a cluster of evil players. Acts on the first night only. Pairs are counted, not individual evil players.

Empath Townsfolk

"Each night, you learn how many of your 2 alive neighbors are evil."

As players die your neighbors change, giving new data each night. A consistent 0 suggests both neighbors are safe; a 2 confirms at least one is almost certainly evil.

Fortune Teller Townsfolk

"Each night, choose 2 players: you learn if either is a Demon. There is a good player who registers as a Demon to you."

One innocent player will always ping as a Demon — your "red herring." Use process of elimination across nights to identify which yes-result is the real Demon.

Investigator Townsfolk

"You start knowing that 1 of 2 players is a particular Minion."

First-night only. You learn a specific Minion type and two candidates. One of them really is that Minion. Comparing with the Librarian's Outsider info can narrow suspects.

Librarian Townsfolk

"You start knowing that 1 of 2 players is a particular Outsider — or that no Outsiders are in play."

First-night only. Useful for confirming or ruling out Drunk/Outsider claims. If the Storyteller says zero Outsiders, take that at face value.

Monk Townsfolk

"Each night*, choose a player (not yourself): they are safe from the Demon tonight."

Acts every night except the first. Protect high-value information roles. If your protected player still dies, a Minion may have caused the kill, or you were poisoned.

Mayor Townsfolk

"If only 3 players live and no execution occurs, your team wins. If you die at night, another player might die instead."

A powerful late-game win condition — the good team can skip the final execution and win outright. The Demon will try to kill you early; your night-death redirect is chosen by the Storyteller.

Oracle Townsfolk

"Each night*, you learn how many dead players are evil."

Tracks the proportion of evil players in the graveyard over time. If the count doesn't rise after an execution, the executed player was probably good.

Seamstress Townsfolk

"Once per game, at night, choose 2 players (not yourself): you learn if they are the same alignment."

One-shot ability. Best used mid-game when you have two suspects. A "same" result on a known-good player confirms the other is also good — or reveals a lying Minion pair.

Dreamer Townsfolk

"Each night, choose a player (not yourself or Travellers): you learn 1 good & 1 evil character, 1 of which is correct."

You always get exactly one true answer among the two shown. Cross-reference with other players' role claims — if neither character is plausible, one is bluffing.

Outsiders — Good (kind of)

Outsiders are on the good team but their abilities often hinder it. Evil may add extra Outsiders via certain Minion roles. Watch out — an Outsider claim can be a Minion bluff.

Drunk Outsider

"You do not know you are the Drunk. You think you are a Townsfolk character, but your ability malfunctions."

The Drunk believes they are a different (valid) Townsfolk role and receives false or misleading information all game. Any claim a Drunk makes is sincere but unreliable. If you suspect someone is Drunk, treat their info with caution.

Minions — Evil

Minions know the Demon and each other from night 1. Their job is to disrupt, deceive, and protect the Demon until evil reaches a winning position.

Poisoner Minion

"Each night, choose a player: they are poisoned tonight and tomorrow day. Their ability malfunctions."

The most disruptive Minion — corrupts Townsfolk information silently. Targets high-value roles like Fortune Teller or Empath. Poison wears off before the following night.

Witch Minion

"Each night, choose a player: if they nominate tomorrow, they die. If only 3 players live, this ability has no effect."

Creates a chilling effect — cursed players fear nominating. The Witch loses their power in the endgame (3 players) to avoid a guaranteed evil win by silencing nominations.

Scarlet Woman Minion

"If there are 5 or more players alive and the Demon dies, you become the Demon."

Evil's insurance policy. Good executing the Demon mid-game may hand evil a fresh Demon. The Scarlet Woman is most dangerous when their existence is unknown. Does not trigger below 5 alive players.

Cerenovus Minion

"Each night, choose a player & a good character: they are 'mad' about being that character until dusk. If they are not mad, they might be executed."

Forces a player to publicly claim a specific role or risk being executed by the Storyteller. This can blow cover for real Townsfolk or force weird claims that cast suspicion. The Storyteller decides if the madness penalty applies.

Demons — Evil

There is one Demon per game. It wins when only 2 players remain. It kills each night and must stay hidden. The good team's primary goal is to identify and execute it.

Imp Demon

"Each night*, choose a player: they die. If you kill yourself this way, a Minion becomes the Imp."

The classic Demon. Can "jump" to a Minion if cornered — a Minion becoming Imp can completely reset good's deductions. The jump is a powerful strategy when the Imp suspects they are identified. Acts every night except the first.
